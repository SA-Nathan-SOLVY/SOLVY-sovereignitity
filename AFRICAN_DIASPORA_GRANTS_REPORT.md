# AFRICAN DIASPORA GRANTS REPORT
## For SOLVY Ecosystem™: Cooperative Neobank for Marginalized Communities

**Prepared:** March 29, 2026  
**Focus:** African immigrants, Caribbean diaspora, Afro-Latino communities, ADOS cooperative economic development  
**Mission Alignment:** Generational wealth for families who survived slavery, displacement, and colonialism

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report identifies **10 high-priority grant opportunities** for the SOLVY Ecosystem, targeting African diaspora economic empowerment through cooperative ownership models. The grants span federal agencies (CDFI Fund, MBDA, SBA), private foundations (Black-led and immigrant-focused), and international development institutions (UNCDF, IFC).

**Key Finding:** The intersectional focus on Black + immigrant status is increasingly recognized by funders, particularly in the cooperative development, refugee economic integration, and racial justice sectors.

#### 1. **CDFI Fund - Financial Assistance Awards**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Funder** | U.S. Department of Treasury, CDFI Fund |
| **Amount** | $500,000 - $5,000,000+ |
| **Deadline** | Annual (typically Spring - check grants.gov) |
| **Eligibility** | Certified CDFIs or CDFI applicants serving low-income communities |

**Mission Alignment:**
- **HIGH ALIGNMENT** with SOLVY's cooperative banking model
- CDFIs specifically serve communities excluded from mainstream banking
- African immigrants and Black communities are primary target populations for CDFI lending

**Application Strategy:**
- Pursue certification as a Community Development Financial Institution
- Emphasize refugee/immigrant lending track record (IRC-CEO model shows 96% repayment rates for refugee borrowers)
- Highlight Islamic-compliant financing (Murabaha) capabilities for African Muslim communities
- Partner with existing CDFIs (e.g., Accompany Capital, IRC-CEO) as a technical assistance provider or referral network

**Key Data Points:**
- Over 1,400 CDFIs manage $222+ billion nationally
- CDFI clients are 85% low-income, 66% people of color
- Refugee-focused CDFIs demonstrate exceptional repayment rates (96-98%)

## KEY SUCCESS FACTORS

### Messaging Framework

**Core Narrative:**
> "SOLVY is building generational wealth for families who survived slavery, displacement, and colonialism through cooperative ownership—a model that keeps wealth circulating within Black, immigrant, and Indigenous communities rather than extracting it to Wall Street."

**Key Statistics to Include:**
- 160 million Africans in diaspora, remitting $96 billion annually (2021)
- Refugees start businesses at higher rates than non-refugee immigrants and US-born
- CDFI refugee lending shows 96-98% repayment rates (defying negative perceptions)
- Black-led CDFIs have smaller, slower-growing balance sheets than white counterparts (Hope Policy Institute)

### Coalition Building

**Strategic Partners:**
- **Collective Diaspora** (Omar Freilla) - Black cooperative network
- **U.S. Federation of Worker Cooperatives** - technical assistance
- **African Development Center** (various cities) - referral network
- **IRC Center for Economic Opportunity** - CDFI best practices
- **Refugee Women's Network** (Atlanta) - partnership model

## CONCLUSION

The funding landscape for African diaspora economic empowerment is **expanding rapidly**, with particular growth in:

1. **Intersectional funding** (Black + immigrant status)
2. **Cooperative development** (worker ownership, community wealth building)
3. **Refugee/immigrant financial inclusion** (proven high repayment rates)
4. **Digital finance for migrants** (remittance-linked products)

SOLVY's **multiracial cooperative model** positions it uniquely to access funding across racial justice, immigrant integration, and cooperative development portfolios simultaneously.

**Recommended Immediate Action:** Apply for CDFI certification and contact the Black Renaissance Fund to initiate conversations about the cooperative neobank model for African diaspora wealth building.
