## 2. FIRST CIRCLE MEMBERSHIP FRAMEWORK

### 2.1 First Circle Definition
The "First Circle" represents the founding cohort of 100 members who establish the cooperative's baseline capital, governance precedents, and economic model. These members receive:

- **Founding Member Status**: Permanent recognition in cooperative governance
- **Priority Dividend Rights**: First priority in 70% member dividend pool
- **Constitutional Convention Rights**: Voting rights on amendments for first 24 months
- **Legacy Rate Lock**: $100 equity buy-in (future members may pay higher rates)

### 2.2 Membership Eligibility Criteria

#### Tier 1: IBC Practitioners (Priority Access)
- Licensed cosmetologists, estheticians, or nail technicians
- Currently operating as independent contractors or salon owners
- Active business registration (DBA, LLC, or sole proprietorship)
- Physical address in Texas (Phase 1), or other US state (Phase 2+)

#### Tier 2: Beauty Industry Vendors
- Licensed distributors of beauty supplies
- Verified supplier relationships with IBC practitioners
- Annual revenue >$50,000
- References from 3+ IBC practitioners

#### Tier 3: Aligned Service Providers
- Financial advisors specializing in IBC sector
- Business coaches for beauty professionals
- Legal/compliance professionals serving cooperatives
- Referral from 2+ existing First Circle members

### 2.3 Membership Exclusions
- **Prohibited**: MSBs, money transmitters, or crypto exchanges (regulatory risk)
- **Prohibited**: Businesses with >25% revenue from cash-only operations (compliance risk)
- **Prohibited**: Individuals on OFAC sanctions lists or with banking fraud history
- **Restricted**: Businesses with prior AML/BSA violations (case-by-case review)

### 2.4 Member Onboarding Process

#### Step 1: Application (Digital)
- Online application via SOLVY portal
- KYB/KYC documentation upload:
  - Government-issued ID
  - Business license (if applicable)
  - EIN confirmation letter or SSN (for sole props)
  - Proof of address (utility bill, lease)
- Membership tier selection
- Referral code (if applicable)

#### Step 2: Verification (Automated + Manual)
- **Automated**: Unit.co identity verification API
- **Automated**: OFAC/sanctions screening
- **Manual**: Business license verification (48-hour SLA)
- **Manual**: Reference checks for Tier 2/3 (if applicable)

#### Step 3: Equity Contribution
- $100 equity buy-in via ACH or debit card
- Held in escrow until membership approval
- Refundable if application rejected (minus $10 processing fee)

#### Step 4: Constitutional Ratification
- Digital signature on cooperative operating agreement
- Acknowledgment of 70/20/10 economic model
- Agreement to one-member-one-vote governance

#### Step 5: Account Activation
- SOLVY Card issuance (physical + virtual)
- Access to member dashboard
- Onboarding to cooperative governance platform
- Assignment to First Circle cohort

## 3. GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

### 3.1 Member Rights

#### Economic Rights
- **Dividend Participation**: Pro-rata share of 70% member dividend pool
- **Patronage Refunds**: Distribution of surplus based on transaction volume
- **Equity Redemption**: Right to redeem equity upon exit (12-month lockup)
- **Asset Claim**: Proportional claim on cooperative assets upon dissolution

#### Governance Rights
- **Voting**: One member, one vote on all constitutional matters
- **Board Election**: Vote for 5-member Member Representative Board
- **Amendment Approval**: Vote on changes to this Constitution (2/3 majority required)
- **Information Access**: Right to inspect financial records and board minutes

#### Operational Rights
- **Product Access**: Full access to SOLVY Card and cooperative services
- **Network Participation**: Access to member directory and referral network
- **Educational Resources**: Free access to DECIDEY NGO financial literacy programs
- **Priority Support**: Dedicated support channel for First Circle members

### 3.2 Member Representative Board

#### Composition
- **5 Members**: Elected by First Circle for 2-year terms
- **Staggered Elections**: 2-3 seats up for election annually
- **Term Limits**: Maximum 2 consecutive terms (4 years)
- **Compensation**: Board service is volunteer; reimbursement for expenses only

#### Powers
- Operational oversight of SA Nathan LLC
- Hiring/firing of executive management
- Budget approval and financial oversight
- Strategic partnership approvals
- Amendment recommendations to membership

#### Limitations
- Cannot modify 70/20/10 economic model without 75% member approval
- Cannot issue new equity classes without constitutional amendment
- Cannot dissolve cooperative without 90% member approval
- Must maintain minimum 3:1 ratio of practitioner to non-practitioner board members

### 3.3 Decision-Making Protocols

#### Day-to-Day Operations
- **Authority**: Executive management (Sean Marlon II, CEO)
- **Scope**: Transaction processing, customer service, routine compliance
- **Oversight**: Monthly reporting to Board

#### Strategic Decisions
- **Authority**: Member Representative Board
- **Scope**: Banking partner changes, major contracts, executive hiring
- **Process**: Board majority vote (3 of 5)

#### Constitutional Amendments
- **Authority**: Full membership vote
- **Scope**: Changes to this document, equity structure, dissolution
- **Process**: 
  1. Proposal by 10% of members OR Board majority
  2. 30-day notice to all members
  3. 2/3 majority vote required
  4. Digital voting platform with audit trail

#### Emergency Powers
- **Authority**: CEO + Board Chair (joint)
- **Scope**: Fraud response, regulatory emergency, system security
- **Limitations**: 72-hour duration; must seek member ratification within 7 days

### 3.4 Meeting Requirements

#### Annual Member Assembly
- **Frequency**: Once per year (Q1)
- **Format**: Hybrid (in-person + virtual)
- **Quorum**: 51% of active members
- **Agenda**: Financial reports, board elections, constitutional amendments

## 4. ECONOMIC MODEL: 70/20/10

### 4.1 Revenue Sources
Primary revenue from SOLVY Card interchange fees (estimated 1.2-1.5% of transaction volume).

### 4.2 Distribution Mechanism

#### 70% - Member Dividend Pool
- **Eligibility**: Active members with >$100/month in card transactions
- **Calculation**: Pro-rata based on transaction volume (not equity)
- **Distribution**: Quarterly, via ACH to member SOLVY accounts
- **Example**: $100,000 monthly interchange = $70,000 pool ÷ eligible members

#### 20% - Community Development Pool
- **Purpose**: Collective benefit projects voted by membership
- **Examples**: Group insurance purchasing, shared marketing, bulk supply discounts
- **Governance**: Proposed by any member; approved by simple majority
- **Transparency**: Public ledger of all pool expenditures

#### 10% - Operations & Reserves
- **Administrative Costs**: Unit.co fees, compliance, technology
- **Regulatory Reserve**: Minimum 6 months operating expenses
- **Growth Capital**: Product development, marketing, expansion

### 4.3 Patronage vs. Equity Returns

#### Patronage Dividends (Primary)
- Based on **use** of cooperative services (transaction volume)
- Distributed annually (Form 1099-PATR)
- Taxed as ordinary income to members
- Aligns incentives: more transactions = more dividends

#### Equity Returns (Secondary)
- 4% annual dividend on $100 equity contribution (if surplus allows)
- Cumulative but subordinate to patronage
- Paid only after patronage distributions and reserve requirements

### 4.4 Capitalization & Reserves

#### Initial Capitalization (First Circle)
- 100 members × $100 = $10,000 initial equity
- Purpose: Seed regulatory reserve, Unit.co setup costs

#### Ongoing Capital Requirements
- **Regulatory Reserve**: Minimum $50,000 by Month 12
- **Growth Reserve**: 10% of interchange revenue retained quarterly
- **Emergency Fund**: $25,000 accessible for fraud/regulatory emergencies

## 5. COMPLIANCE & REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

### 5.1 Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Program

#### Customer Identification Program (CIP)
- **Identity Verification**: Unit.co automated KYB/KYC
- **Document Retention**: 5 years post-account closure
- **Risk Rating**: Each member assigned risk score (low/medium/high)
- **Enhanced Due Diligence**: Required for high-risk members

#### Suspicious Activity Monitoring
- **Automated**: Unit.co transaction monitoring
- **Manual**: Monthly review of flagged transactions
- **Reporting**: SAR filing within 30 days of detection
- **Recordkeeping**: 5-year retention

#### OFAC Compliance
- **Screening**: Real-time sanctions list screening
- **Updates**: Daily OFAC list updates
- **Blocking**: Immediate freeze on sanctioned parties
- **Reporting**: Within 10 business days to OFAC

### 5.2 BSA/AML Officer
- **Designation**: [Sean Marlon II or appointed officer]
- **Responsibilities**:
  - AML program oversight
  - SAR filing authority
  - Regulatory examination coordination
  - Annual AML training for all staff
- **Independence**: Reports directly to Board, not operational management

### 5.3 Information Security

#### Data Protection
- **Encryption**: AES-256 for data at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit
- **Access Control**: Role-based access control (RBAC)
- **Breach Response**: 72-hour notification to affected members
- **Audit Trail**: Immutable logs of all administrative actions

#### Member Privacy
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only necessary data
- **Consent**: Explicit consent for data sharing
- **Right to Deletion**: Members can request account deletion (post-compliance period)
- **Third Parties**: Unit.co only; no data sales

### 5.4 State Licensing

#### Current Requirements (Texas)
- **MSB Registration**: Required if >$1,000/day in transmission
- **Money Transmitter License**: Not required for card issuing (bank partner handles)
- **Sales Tax**: Exempt (financial services)

#### Expansion Requirements
- State-by-state analysis required before expansion
- Additional MSB registrations as needed
- Legal counsel review for each new state

#### Member Exit
- **Voluntary**: 30-day notice; equity refunded after 12-month lockup
- **Involuntary**: Fraud, sanctions violations, or material breach of Constitution
- **Equity Treatment**: Prorated refund minus any losses attributable to member

#### Cooperative Dissolution
- **Trigger**: 90% member vote OR regulatory order
- **Process**: 
  1. Cease new transactions
  2. Return member equity
  3. Distribute remaining assets pro-rata
  4. File dissolution documents
- **Timeline**: 180 days maximum
