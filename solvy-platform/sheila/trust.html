Article 1: Establishing the Trust

Section 1.01 — Funding and Source

The Grantor transfers $250,000 to this trust. This transfer is a gift from a mother to her disabled adult child under the disabled child exception (42 U.S.C. § 1396p(c)(2)(B)(iii) and SSA POMS SI 01150.121A.2). This transfer is excepted from transfer penalties and shall not affect the Grantor's SSI or Medicaid eligibility.

Section 1.02 — Trust Type

This trust is a first-party Supplemental Needs Trust under 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4)(A). The beneficiary, Sean Maurice Mayo, is a disabled individual under age 65 who receives SSI, VA disability benefits, and TDIU.

Section 1.03 — Irrevocability

This trust is irrevocable. The Grantor cannot alter, amend, revoke, or terminate it.

Article 2: Beneficiary & Distributions

Section 2.01 — Sole Lifetime Beneficiary

Sean Maurice Mayo is the sole lifetime beneficiary.

Section 2.02 — Distributions

The Trustee shall make no direct cash distributions to the beneficiary. All distributions shall be made directly to third-party vendors for the beneficiary's supplemental needs, including but not limited to:

  • Medical and dental care not covered by public benefits
  • Mental health services
  • Transportation (vehicle purchase, insurance, maintenance, gas)
  • Home modifications
  • Furniture, appliances, electronics
  • Recreation, vacations, hobbies
  • Education and vocational training
  • Personal care attendants
  • Phone, internet, and computer equipment

The Trustee shall not pay for food or shelter items that would reduce the beneficiary's SSI benefits, unless properly reported in accordance with SSA rules.

Article 3: Trustee & Trust Protector

Section 3.01 — Trustee

The initial Trustee is Sean Marlon II McDaniel. The Trustee has full authority to manage trust assets, make discretionary distributions, file tax returns, and administer the trust. Sean Maurice Mayo shall not serve as Trustee or Co-Trustee.

Section 3.02 — Trust Protector

The Trust Protector is Sean Maurice Mayo. The Trust Protector has the authority to:

  • Remove and appoint successor Trustees
  • Change the governing law or situs of administration
  • Amend administrative provisions to preserve benefits eligibility or comply with changes in law

The Trust Protector shall have no day-to-day management authority and no authority to direct distributions or investments.

Article 4: Medicaid Payback

Upon the death of Sean Maurice Mayo, the Trustee shall reimburse the state Medicaid agency for benefits paid on his behalf, as required under 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4)(A). After such reimbursement, any remaining trust assets shall be distributed to the remainder beneficiaries named in Schedule A.

Article 5: Spendthrift Provision

This trust is a spendthrift trust. No beneficiary may assign, anticipate, encumber, or transfer any interest in the trust's income or principal. Trust assets shall not be subject to attachment, bankruptcy, or any other legal process.
