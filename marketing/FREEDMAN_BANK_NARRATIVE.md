## The Original Promise (1865)

On March 3, 1865 — the same month the Civil War ended — Congress chartered the **Freedman's Savings and Trust Company**, commonly called the Freedman's Bank.

### The Vision
- **For:** Formerly enslaved African Americans
- **Mission:** "To instill into the minds of the untutored Africans lessons of sobriety, wisdom, and economy"
- **Promise:** A secure place to save, build wealth, and participate in the American economy
- **Branches:** 37 locations across 17 states
- **Depositors:** Over 61,000 Black Americans
- **Deposits:** $57 million (in 1870s dollars)

### The Betrayal
The bank's white trustees — including Henry Cooke (brother of Treasury Secretary Jay Cooke) — invested depositor money recklessly:
- Real estate speculation
- Railroad bonds
- Personal loans to bank officers

**No Black Americans served on the board.** The people whose money built the institution had no voice in how it was managed.

### The Collapse (1874)
- The Panic of 1873 triggered bank failures nationwide
- Freedman's Bank could not meet withdrawal demands
- **61,000 depositors lost their savings**
- Frederick Douglass personally donated his own money trying to save it — and lost everything he put in

### Frederick Douglass's Words
> "The whole thing was beautiful. I had read about this bank when I was a slave, and I had very properly associated it with the emancipation of my race... But I found that it was not what I thought it was."
